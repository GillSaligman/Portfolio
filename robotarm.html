7-DOF Robotic Arm

NASA RASC-AL | 2024 – 2025

Project Overview

This project is a modular robotic arm mounted to a custom motorized chassis, designed for autonomous surface exploration. Built for the NASA RASC-AL competition, it represents a full-stack engineering effort — I designed the mechanical structure, selected and integrated the electronics, developed embedded firmware, and built a ROS2 communication system.

Project Highlights

Team and Final Prototype

Our team worked collaboratively to design and build the final system. I was responsible for the design, prototyping, coding, and systems integration of the arm and controller.

Design, Code & Prototyping

I led design calculations, developed the firmware and hardware architecture, and iteratively prototyped each joint using 3D-printed components.

Software and Control

ROS2 coordinates communication between a controller, the Raspberry Pi, and six ESP-based joints. Each joint runs its own PID controller with onboard feedback, while the central system manages coordination, user input, and simulation in Gazebo.

Electronics

Each joint is independently powered and contains an ESP8266, motor driver, potentiometer, and local PID logic. The distributed architecture ensures modularity and minimizes wiring complexity while enhancing fault isolation.

Mechanical Design

Every joint was custom designed and torque-calculated to meet task specifications. We used aluminum tubing and 3D-printed clamps for strength, precision, and weight savings. Internal routing and modular joinery support field servicing.

Prototyping and Assembly

The modular architecture allowed us to prototype each joint independently, ensuring rapid debugging and minimal integration overhead. Since each joint communicates with the Raspberry Pi in parallel, subsystem failures do not cascade — similar to octopus limbs. Once validated, the system worked seamlessly when combined.

Takeaways and Future Work

The project will be passed to future Drexel students. Recommendations include drivetrain upgrades, completing J1/J2, and better terrain traversal. A master documentation package was created to ease future onboarding and iteration.
