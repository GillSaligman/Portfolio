My Role at Nauticus Robotics

As a mechanical engineer at Nauticus Robotics, I stepped into the shoes of a full-time engineer just two weeks into my internship. During a critical restructuring period, I helped prepare the Aquanaut MKII — an autonomous subsea robot — for deep water testing, working long hours to meet strict deadlines. I created detailed assembly guides, replaced corroded components, solved routing issues for hydraulic lines, and built a prototype master controller for the Olympic Arm.

My most valuable contribution came from combining knowledge across mechanical design, systems understanding, and documentation. From interpreting CAD assemblies to physically modifying subcomponents under pressure, I brought clarity and action to a project that demanded both. I left the company with a working controller prototype and a deeper understanding of the demands of real-world robotics engineering.
