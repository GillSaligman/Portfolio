Nauticus Robotics

Drexel Co-Op | September 2023 – April 2024

My Role at Nauticus Robotics

As a mechanical engineer at Nauticus Robotics, I stepped into the role of a full-time engineer just two weeks into my internship. I supported vehicle readiness for deep-sea testing by replacing hardware, solving oil routing issues, and finalizing system configurations under strict timelines. I contributed to both the Aquanaut MKII and the Olympic Arm platforms during a fast-paced restructuring period, demonstrating flexibility and hands-on problem-solving in a high-pressure environment.

In Action

Preparing for Trials

Swapping out mission-critical parts on the vehicle and arm to prepare for deep-sea trials. Due to strict delivery deadlines, I worked overtime on-site to ensure the vehicle passed pressure tests and was fully mission-ready.

Olympic Arm Controller

One of several projects I supported — I led development of a master controller for the Olympic Arm. I drafted specs, interviewed ROV operators, pitched designs, sourced electronics, and built a functional prototype that interfaced with a simulator — all in the final three weeks of my internship.
