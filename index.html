Engineering Portfolio

Jesse Gill Saligman | Mechanical Engineering @ Drexel University

Nauticus Robotics

As a mechanical engineering intern at Nauticus Robotics, I took on full engineer responsibilities during a high-pressure restructuring period. I contributed to the design, assembly, and testing of critical components for the Aquanaut MKII and the Olympic Arm, including solving mechanical challenges and designing a prototype master controller for advanced manipulator control. This experience sharpened my ability to lead, adapt, and deliver under demanding conditions.

7-DOF Robotic Arm

For my senior design project, I led the development of a modular 7-DOF robotic arm with fully distributed control. Each joint features a microcontroller and closed-loop PID control via Wi-Fi, all coordinated through a ROS2-based architecture. I handled mechanical design, electronics, and software—building a system that was as scalable as it was precise.

FIRST Robotics Competition

From 2017–2021, I competed in FRC as a mechanical lead, robot operator, and team captain. I led rapid prototyping efforts, implemented design improvements under time constraints, and led my team to awards for innovation. My biggest growth came from iterative problem-solving, hands-on leadership, and engineering under pressure.

About Me

I’m Jesse Gill Saligman, a mechanical engineering student at Drexel University with a passion for robotics, system integration, and rapid prototyping. From building robots that competed internationally to creating subsea manipulators and ROS2-integrated arms, I bring a hands-on approach to engineering that emphasizes real-world problem-solving and systems thinking. Whether in a lab, at competition, or working under pressure in the field—I build to learn and learn to build.
