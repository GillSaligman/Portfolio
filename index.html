Nauticus Robotics

As a mechanical engineer at Nauticus Robotics, I took on full-time responsibilities during a critical restructuring phase. I contributed to the design, manufacturing, and assembly of components for the Aquanaut MKII and Olympic Arm, solved oil pressure and hose routing challenges, and built a master controller prototype for advanced arm control. I worked long hours to meet tight deadlines and ensure the vehicle was ready for deep water testing.

7-DOF Robotic Arm

This project is a modular robotic arm mounted to a custom motorized chassis, designed for autonomous surface exploration. It was built for the NASA RASC-AL competition and represents a full-stack engineering effort — I calculated joint torques, selected and integrated components, and developed everything from mechanical structure to embedded firmware and ROS2 communication protocols.

FIRST Robotics Competition

As a former team captain, mechanical lead, and robot operator in FRC, I designed and iterated advanced competitive robots under tight timelines. Highlights include a turret-based dodgeball shooter with a rotating magazine and a full robot redesign completed in under a week. Today, I continue mentoring FRC students, helping them explore and grow in STEM fields.
